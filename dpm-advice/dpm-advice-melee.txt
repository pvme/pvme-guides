## __Weapon Camping__
.tag:camping
> *"Camping"* means to use one weapon/set for all attacks.
> *"Juggling"* means to switch between gear to maximise damage output.

Although camping has become far more common with recent powercreep, for most situations juggling is still recommended to maximise damage output.

In you want to weapon camp (for example if you have accessibility considerations or you are AFKing), use the following lists to decided which is best for your situation:

## __Critical Strikes and 'Crit Builds'__
.tag:crits
With the exception of the Equilibrium Aura <:equilibriumaura:1233722649834684416> which prevents them, **critical strikes** (or "crits") are an important but commonly misunderstood way of improving your damage output.

.
### __What is a critical strike?__
All attacks have a 10% chance to crit, which increases the damage of that attack based on your <:strength:689509250879324165> level (*not* <:attack:689509250472476758>).

Here's a worked example:

> You use an ability which would normally deal 2,000 base damage.
> You are level 90 <:strength:689509250879324165>, therefore +50% damage on-crit.
> You successfully land a crit, so +1,000 crit damage is added (+50% of 2,000).
> You now deal 3,000 damage (2,000 + 1,000).

.

### __Step 2: Harnessing your crits__
We can go one step further with crits, leveraging the effects of **Meteor Strike** <:meteorstrike:535532879359377439> , **Incendiary Shot** <:incend:535541258429661215> and **Tsunami** <:tsunami:535533809995874304> to generate additional adrenaline any time you crit for 30.6 seconds after using the ability - shown on your buff bar as <:critbuff:1279516151423963166>.

Below are two examples of ways we can generate a large amount of adrenaline in a short time whilst under these abilities' effects:
⬥ Switch to a range weapon with Caroming 4 and cast **Greater Ricochet** <:gricocaroming:867678153966878740>
⬥ Switch to a mage weapon and cast **Greater Conc** <:gconc:869285393223254107>

Now we have an excess amount of adrenaline and can use it to do large amounts of damage in a short space of time. Common ways to "dump" excess adrenaline include:
⬥ **Dragon Claws Spec** <:dragonclaw:779048041088024606>
⬥ Early use of **Berserk** <:zerk:535532854004678657> or **EZK spec** <:zekkil:903244090953588787>
    • Note: can mess up your rotations due to cooldowns.
⬥ **Zaros Godsword Spec** <:zgs:626465964325601290>
⬥ **Natural Instinct** <:nat:535541258131865633>
    • Note: allows for even more adrenaline gain!

## __Cancelling Channeled Abilities__
.tag:channels
Some abilities known as "channels" take longer than other abilities to complete. Examples for melee include <:assault:535532855191928842> <:gflurry:535532879283879977> <:destroy:535532879330148352>.

By learning to cancel these channels at the right time, you can avoid lost ticks and therefore damage potential. Reasons for needing to cancel include:
⬥ Cancelling channelled abilities saves time.
⬥ Animations are sometimes unsynced with the hitsplats.
⬥ It can be higher damage-per-tick and more adrenaline friendly to cancel early.

.
See the following example with <:conc:1087373968731811880> from Magic (functionally identical to <:fury:535532879510372352>):
.img:https://img.pvme.io/images/9C2JEZQ.mp4

.
### __How do I cancel channels?__
Watch: [when to cancel Melee abilities](<https://youtu.be/y33z3WLM7H8>).

⬥ **Assault** <:assault:535532855191928842> / **Destroy** <:destroy:535532879330148352> / **Greater Flurry** <:gflurry:535532879283879977>
    • Cancel on 4th hit (7t/4.2s), or after 2 or 3 hits.
    • *Note on <:assault:535532855191928842>: hit timings vary with DW vs. 2H, but cancel timings stay the same—DW shifts hits by 1 tick, but cancel times are unaffected.*
    • *Note on <:destroy:535532879330148352>: use 4 hits to speed up <:zerk:535532854004678657> cooldown or adjust as needed for rotation alignment.*

## __Indirect Damage Boosts__
.tag:indirect
In addition to abilities and attacks, there are a few notable supporting buffs you can use to further boost your DPM:

⬥ **Ruby Aurora** <:rubyaurora:574292444791963659> (Ancient Spellbook) - 5% chance (10% with Light Form) to grant players around the caster (not the caster themself) +1% damage boost for 24s, stacking up to +3%.
⬥ **Weapon specs** <:spec:537340400273195028> are covered in later.

## __Putting It Together (Building a Rotation)__
.tag:rotations
By now you should understand:
⬥ How to build **adrenaline** and which basic abilites to use.
⬥ Why **crits** are so important and how to harness them.
⬥ **Indirect** ways to improve your damage output.
⬥ How and why to cancel **channels**.

While melee rotations vary by available unlocks and situation/boss, there are two duration-based types:
1. **"Burst Damage"** - For short kills or hybrid scenarios, focus on maximising quick, high-damage abilities.
2. **"Sustained Damage"** - For longer fights, aim to keep consistent damage output, cycling abilities efficiently.

.
### __Maximising Use of Berserk__
.tag:rotations_berserk
Most rotations are built around the **Berserk** <:zerk:535532854004678657> ultimate ability, used as often as possible to leverage its +100% damage boost.

Whichever rotation followed, your <:zerk:535532854004678657> rotation will come in 2 phases:
⬥ **Phase 1 - Berserk active**
    • Use high-damage thresholds and special attacks
    • Avoid bleeds within Berserk, as they aren’t boosted
        ⬩ Dismember <:dismember:535532879376023572> may be used for adrenaline gain if no stronger abilities are available
⬥ **Phase 2 - Berserk on cooldown**
    • Build adrenaline and alternate with specials to avoid camping at 100% adrenaline

> Note: 10 abilities fit within Berserk’s 20.4s duration (increased to 14 abilities over 26.4s with 3 pieces of Vestments of Havoc <:vestmentsofhavochood:994189297659940904>).

.

### __Greater Fury__
.tag:gfury
__How it works__
⬥ Greater Fury <:gfury:535532879334080527> is an upgraded version of regular Fury <:fury:535532879510372352>, unlocked by reading a Greater Fury ability codex <:gbargecodex:1004453769112735764> costing <:coins:698816156961603654> $data_pvme:unlock_gfury$.
⬥ Greater Fury <:gfury:535532879334080527> hits **once** dealing 157% ability damage, giving the next ability an added 10% critical strike chance.
    • Switching your main hand weapon loses the buff.
    • **If <:gfury:535532879334080527> crits, the first hit of the next ability will be a guaranteed crit.**
    • As it becomes a non-channelled ability, it is Revolution-friendly.

.
__Is it worth it?__
⬥ **Generally, Greater Fury is not a recommended upgrade**.
    • Regular Fury <:fury:535532879510372352> has better synergy with the Abyssal Scourge <:abyssalscourge:947871842469834832> in a fight
⬥ It is *possible* for <:gfury:535532879334080527> to be stronger (on average) than some 188% damage abilities ( <:cleave:535532878616985610> / <:sever:535532879577612298> / <:deci:535532879325822986> ) under the right circumstances.
    • This is due to its potential guaranteed crit buffs.
    • This requires sufficient critical chance stacking, using a Grimoire <:grim:568262896375824385> and Biting 4 <:biting4:712073087809617931> perk active, *and* the next ability must be both high-damage and be able to crit.
    • Some examples of follow-up abilities include Igneous Overpower <:overpowerigneous:959089455640215633>, charged Greater Barge <:gbarge:535532879250456578> and Assault <:assault:535532855191928842>.

### Greater Barge
.tag:gbarge
__How it works__
⬥ Greater Barge <:gbarge:535532879250456578> is an upgraded version of regular Barge <:barge:535532853916860437>, unlocked by reading a Greater Fury ability codex <:gbargecodex:1004453769112735764> costing <:coins:698816156961603654> $data_pvme:unlock_gbarge$.
⬥ It has two components - its own damage, and its passive effect.
⬥ Normal ability effect:
    • For each tick (0.6s) since a player stopped attacking their target, <:gbarge:535532879250456578> damage range increases (+2% minimum hit, +10% maximum hit), capping at 10 ticks for a damage range of 45%-225%.
    • It also clears binds on the player, and where possible, will also bind a target for 11t/6.6s.
.
⬥ Additionally, if it has been **at least 8t/4.8s since the player has attacked a target**, it gains another effect:
    • This is indicated by the Greater Barge <:gbarge:535532879250456578> icon appearing on your Buff Bar.
    • Using the ability will cause a second buff icon <:voke:535541259465392143> to appear on your bar.
    • **The next following melee channelled ability within 10t/6s will become a damage-over-time (DoT) instead**.
    • **This is the reason <:gbarge:535532879250456578> is so strong.**
.
*Note: Channelled abilities that become DoTs through the effect of <:gbarge:535532879250456578> are typically called barge "bleeds" - however, they are **not** actually bleeds.*

.
__Is it worth it?__
⬥ Being able to **turn channelled abilities into DoTs** offers the following advantages:
    • It reduces the time required to cast them to 3t/1.8s, allowing you to continue using other abilities while they deal damage.
    • This is very strong when combined with something like Berserk <:zerk:535532854004678657> to fit more high-damaging abilities into its short duration.
    • It also **increases the burst potential of Melee** <:melee:1096130867279171706> in a fight.
⬥ This effect is typically used at the following places:
    • At the start of a fight.
    • After any mechanic that forces you to stop attacking for a period of time (e.g. reflect at Vorago).
    • After a Berserk <:zerk:535532854004678657> mid-combat.

__Using Greater Barge in Combat__
⬥ To use <:gbarge:535532879250456578> in combat, there are two ways to do it.
⬥ **Method 1**:
    • Ability + click away → any Defensive → <:zerk:535532854004678657> → <:gbarge:535532879250456578>
        ⬩ You must click away at least 1 tick before the defensive, or you will not get the bleed effect.
    • Defensives include abilities such as Anticipation <:anti:535541306475151390> , Freedom <:freedom:535541258240786434>, etc.
⬥ **Method 2**:
    • Ability → wait 2 ticks → <:zerk:535532854004678657> → <:barge:535532853916860437>
        ⬩ Doing it this way saves 1 tick (8t vs 9t) compared to the first method.

.
__Notes__
⬥ Multiple <:gbarge:535532879250456578> DoTs can stack on the same target, so it is group-PvM-friendly.
⬥ Bleeding <:gflurry:535532879283879977> will still reduce the cooldown of <:zerk:535532854004678657> for all 4 hits.
⬥ Unlike normal non-damaging abilities, ZGS special attack <:zgs:626465964325601290> <:spec:537340400273195028> will try to drag you in when using it, so remember to click away as you use it otherwise you will auto-attack and lose the <:gbarge:535532879250456578> buff.
⬥ **\*** Greater Barge <:gbarge:535532879250456578> does not give adrenaline if you still have a target that is dead.
⬥ Damage dealt from the bleed will be affected by the equipment *currently* worn.
    • e.g. if you bleed Assault <:assault:535532855191928842> with T80 weapons and change to T90, any remaining hits will be calculated with T90 damage instead of T80.
⬥ Each bleed hit rolls its own chance to hit.
⬥ If you stall a barge bleed the following will occur:
    • Hit 1 will be stalled, and when released will hit at the time it usually would as the first hit of that ability.
    • Hits 2-4 will *not* be stalled, and will hit the target at their usual timing, *no matter how far you currently are from the target* - this allows the hits to be far-cast.
    • This is possibly a bug.

### Greater Flurry
.tag:gflurry
__How it works__
⬥ Greater Flurry <:gflurry:535532879283879977> is an upgraded version of regular Flurry <:flurry:864492981763702834>, unlocked by reading a Greater Flurry ability codex <:gflurrycodex:1004453767397249144> costing <:coins:698816156961603654> $data_pvme:unlock_gflurry$.
⬥ Each hit of <:gflurry:535532879283879977> reduces the cooldown of Berserk by 2t/1.2s, up to a maximum of 4 hits, or 8t/4.8s.
⬥ The damage dealt depends on the situation:
    • Against a single target, damage is increased, dealing 31-157% damage per hit.
    • Against multiple targets, the damage is the same as normal Flurry <:flurry:864492981763702834>.
    • If bled with <:gbarge:535532879250456578> it does NOT act as an AoE.

.
__Is it worth it?__
⬥ It can help reduce the cooldown time of Berserk <:zerk:535532854004678657> significantly, used almost 3 times per minute. This is important to minimize the amount of time spent outside a damage-boosting ultimate.
⬥ It acts as an extra strong damaging threshold for Melee <:melee:1096130867279171706> users.
⬥ You can cancel it on a desired hit to help line up timings for an ability on the last tick of <:zerk:535532854004678657>.
    • For more information, see `!lasttick` command.

.tag:chaos_roar
*Note: for a quick summary, you can use the `!chaosroar` command.*
__How it works__
⬥ Chaos Roar <:chaosroar:994644356671737966> is a basic ability unlocked by reading a Chaos roar Ability codex <:chaosroarcodex:994189286960287764> costing <:coins:698816156961603654> $data_pvme:unlock_chaosroar$.

    • For channelled abilities like Assault <:assault:535532855191928842> only get their first hit boosted.

    • Ek-Zekkil special attack <:zekkil:903244090953588787> <:eofspec:1257438999794946099> can deal massive damage used after <:chaosroar:994644356671737966>.

## __Defensive Autos__
.tag:defensive_autos
While colloquially referred to as 'defensive' autos, it is more accurate to call them 'non-damaging' autos, since some non-defensive abilities have similar interactions with autos. Examples include ZGS special attack <:zgs:626465964325601290> <:spec:537340400273195028> and Berserk <:zerk:535532854004678657>.
For a thorough explanation of how defensive autos (and auto attacks in general) work, refer to <#1020550458231771157>. Here, we will simply cover some of their uses.
.
### Why Defensive Autos Exist
⬥ Generally, casting any ability will put your auto-attack <:AirSurge:543465115870035999> on cooldown based on your weapon speed.
⬥ However, **non-damaging abilities do not cause this cooldown.**
    • This means that defensive autos can be used within your main ability rotation.
⬥ Note that Melee <:melee:1096130867279171706> does not have an auto attack keybind like Magic <:Magic:689504724159823906> and so some input sequences may be different/delayed.

.
### Common Applications
*Note: You can check out `!defauto` and `!freedom` for a brief summary of mechanics.*
.
⬥ **Forcing 2H autos** using `Fast/Fastest Ability → Defensive → Ability`.
    • Replacing the with a *magic spell keybind* with the appropriate runes will attempt to cast the spell in place of the auto.
⬥ **Forcing off-hand + 2H autos** with Freedom <:freedom:535541258240786434>
    • The rotation is: Fast/Fastest Ability → Freedom → (1t delay) + 2H switch → Ability
    • This will give an off-hand and a 2H auto, but do not switch to 2H too early.
⬥ **Applying Smoke Cloud <:smokecloud:856635090641879050>**
    • **Method 1**: Press <:smokecloud:856635090641879050> + Ability 1 tick after GCD ends to 4TAA <:smokecloud:856635090641879050>.
        ⬩ This must be done following a Fastest speed, non-channelled ability.
    • **Method 2**: Use <:freedom:535541258240786434> to get OH + 2H autos, and replace the OH auto by pressing <:smokecloud:856635090641879050> before <:freedom:535541258240786434> - this is 1t faster than method 1.
        ⬩ This requires a magic off-hand with an autocast set, that you have the required runes for.
    • **Method 3**: Press Target Cycle → <:smokecloud:856635090641879050> + Ability to start an encounter if you do not intend to utilise the Greater Barge <:gbarge:535532879250456578> buff at the start of a fight.
