# Skills Training
.tag:skills
Some skills are a vital part of your character's PvM strength. Here we go over some of the ways to train the skills and what they unlock. **Full skilling guides for every skill can be found on the [Wiki](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Skill_training_guides>).**

.

.tag:pray

⬥ **70 <:prayer:1005450651347730533>** - best DPS prayers on the normal prayer book <:Piety:1215399597581533264> <:Rigour:1215399596239618118> <:augury:1084799720926478437> <:sanctity:1137941000636092467>

⬥ **92-95 <:prayer:1005450651347730533> Curses**
    • Requires completion of [The Temple at Senntisten](<https://runescape.wiki/w/The_Temple_at_Senntisten/Quick_guide>) quest
    • Unlocks <:soulsplit:615613924506599497> which heals you based on damage dealt.
    • Gives access to strong DPS curses <:turmoil:583429936606347280> <:anguish:583429936665198592> <:torment:583429936958930964> <:sorrow:1137941003895046284>, which should be your primary long-term goal.
⬥ Primarily train <:prayer:1005450651347730533> by burying bones with [powder of burials](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Powder_of_burials>) <:powderofburials:989191014738698340>.
⬥ **Suggested <:prayer:1005450651347730533> quests & training**:
    • 1-43: Do: [Restless Ghost](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Restless_ghost_quick_guide>), [Priest in Peril](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Priest_in_Peril/Quick_guide>), and [One Piercing Note](<https://runescape.wiki/w/One_Piercing_Note/Quick_guide>) → [Holy Cithara](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Holy_Cithara#Obtaining_the_Holy_Cithara>) (if Desert Treasure completed) quests and then **[God Statues](<https://runescape.wiki/w/God_Statues>)** monthly D&D. Then:

    • 1-70: Bury dragon bones with <:powderofburials:989191014738698340> active
    • 70-95: Bury dinosaur bones with <:powderofburials:989191014738698340> active

## __Summoning__ <:Summoning:583430259064569856>
.tag:sum
Summoning allows you to summon helpful familiars for to aid you in combat via extra inventory, healing, or dealing damage.
*Note: This assumes you already have the charms needed from other PvM content. The list of familiars suggested below is only a recommendation.*
⬥ Check <:summoning:583430259064569856> [Wiki calculators](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Calculators#Summoning>) for up-to-date pricing.
⬥ The weekly Familiarisation minigame can be beneficial when farming charms (triple charms for up to 40 minutes).
⬥ **Charming potions add +1 charms dropped when killing things**, and are tradeable on the GE - use them when gathering charms for training summoning!
⬥ [Arch glacor 0-1 mechanics](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Arch-Glacor#Normal_mode>) is a low-level boss that drops a lot of blue charms.
⬥ **Suggested pouches to craft**:
    • 1-4 : Do the [Wolf Whistle](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Wolf_Whistle/Quick_guide>) quest
    • 4-16: Dreadfowls
    • 16-32: Granite Crabs
    • 32-46: Honeybadgers
    • 46-49: Pyrelords
    • 49-61: Bloated Leeches
    • 61-64: Smoke Devils or Bloated Leeches
    • 64-74: Stranger Plants or Smoke Devils
    • 74-79: Granite Lobsters
    • 79-89: Moss Titans
    • 89-99: Geyser titans

## __Herblore__ <:Herblore:689554435583508558>
.tag:herb
Herblore <:herblore:689554435583508558> grants you access to untradeable potions such as [Overloads](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Overload>) (96+ <:herblore:689554435583508558>for combat skill boosting, and [adrenaline potions](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Adrenaline_potion#(4)>) (84+ <:herblore:689554435583508558>) for more adrenaline on-demand.
*Note: prices of supplies and potions fluctuate. Making certain potions may even be profitable. Do some research before training.*
⬥ <:herblore:689554435583508558> [Wiki calculators](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Calculators#Herblore>) for research.
⬥ [Warbands](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Warbands>) are one option for relatively fast and free Herblore XP, but may require some time investment to learn, and are located in the Wilderness.
    • unavailable for <:iron:764086050959392778>.
⬥ [Herby Werby](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Herby_Werby>) has no requirements and can be done after visiting [Anachronia](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Anachronia/Quick_guide>) once. Buy [ancient seeds](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Ancient_seed>) to return weekly.
⬥ You can choose to wait for a [DXP (Double XP) weekend](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Double_XP_Live>) to train.
⬥ Always make use of portable wells when making potions for the +10% experience boost.

.
⬥ **Suggested training methods**:
    • 1-38: Do Warbands daily, Herby-Werby weekly or the highest level potion available
    • 38-52: [Prayer potions](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Prayer_potion>)
    • 52-66: [Super Energy potions](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Super_Energy_potion>)
    • 66-84: [Super Defence potions](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Super_Defence_potion>)
    • 84-88: [Adrenaline potions](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Adrenaline_potion>)
    • 88-96: Make ~3000 of each [extreme potion](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Extreme_potion>). Should that not be enough, just make some more adrenaline potions OR more extremes of equal amounts until you hit 96.
    • 96-99: [Overloads](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Overload>)
    • 98-99: [Supreme overloads](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Supreme_overload>)
        ⬩ You can boost to 107 <:herblore:689554435583508558> via pulse core once you get to 99 in order to make [Elder overload potions](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Elder_overload_potion>). These are the best-in-slot potions, and very worth the cost.

## __Slayer__ <:slayer:797896049548066857>
.tag:slay
Slayer <:slayer:797896049548066857> may be less <:combat:797896050370281523> xp/hr than other methods; but comes with other benefits like making some <:coins:698816156961603654>, unlocking new monsters to kill, collecting charms for Summoning <:Summoning:583430259064569856> while training your combat stats all at the same time.

⬥ If you choose to do Slayer, prioritise completing the [Smoking Kills](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Smoking_Kills/Quick_guide>) quest. This doubles your point rewards from tasks. Then remember to quickly get a [Full Slayer Helmet](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Full_slayer_helmet>) for the big accuracy and damage boost it provides for <:magic:689504724159823906> <:range:580168050121113623> <:melee:1096130867279171706>.
⬥ Make sure to use the highest available [Slayer Master](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Slayer_Masters#Overview>) to get the best tasks.
⬥ Early on, you can quickly train slayer by squishing Corrupted Scarabs at Menaphos.
    • See the `soulobby` FC for calls on when scarabs spawn.
⬥ Fast/expensive <:slayer:797896049548066857> training method:
    • Kill [greater demon berserkers](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Greater_demon_berserker>) with [Slayer wildcards](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Slayer_Wildcard>) to 115 <:slayer:797896049548066857>. Safespottable and even at lower levels this is very high xp/h.

## __Crafting__ <:crafting:1039864810239705119>
.tag:craft
80 <:crafting:1039864810239705119> is required for Invention <:invention:689509250946695292> and also up to 85 <:crafting:1039864810239705119> to craft <:necromancy:1148995625896120460> armour.
*Note: gp/xp calculations can be found on the Wiki page for training Crafting.*
⬥ <:crafting:1039864810239705119> [Wiki calculators](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Calculators#Crafting>) for research.
⬥ **Free/slow/cheap training methods**:
    • Level 1+: dungeoneering crafting (craft magic/range armour in Daemonheim, sell them to the smuggler and buy more supplies, repeat).
    • Level 1+: crafting urns
    • Level 1+ weekly clan citadel contribution at loom plot
    • Level 75+: Prifddinas harps
⬥ **Fast/pricey training methods**:
    • Level 20+: Cutting gems (sapphire/ruby/emerald/diamond)
    • Level 54+: Crafting battlestaves
    • Level 63+: Crafting dragonhide armour

## __Invention__ <:Invention:689509250946695292>
.tag:invention
Invention unlocks the ability to use perks on gear, which adds a huge amount of DPS, skilling utility and survivability.

*Note: see <#874326209528479805> for more information.*
⬥ **Suggested training methods**:
    • 1-4: Complete the [Invention tutorial](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Invention_tutorial>).
    • 4-27: Research max item level 5, and disassemble two items at level 5 (cheap options like crystal halberd, black salamander, etc.)
    • 27-60: Research max item level 10, then disassemble items once they hit level 10.
    • 60-99/120: Disassemble (at level 10) or siphon (at level 12) weapons and armour while doing PvM/bossing and the skill will passively level up over time.
⬥ **Shattered Worlds can be used to powerlevel invention, at the cost of combat experience**.
    • See `!shatteredworlds` command for more information.
    • Item experience will be higher than AFK alternatives, but the combat experience will be significantly reduced.

## __Dungeoneering__ <:dungeoneering:1003947795541143632>
.tag:dung
Note: *As tokens are the limiting factor for most upgrades, the farming of <:dungtoken:762960358930907156> is the focus of this subsection.*
Dungeoneering <:dungeoneering:1003947795541143632> is a support skill that gives a plethora of [rewards](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Daemonheim_Rewards>) to enhance your skilling and pvm (e.g. [ring of vigour](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Ring_of_vigour>) and [Charming imp](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Charming_imp>)) that can be bought with <:dungtoken:762960358930907156>.
⬥  [Dungeoneering](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Dungeoneering#Preparing_for_a_dungeon>) can be trained by completing Daemonheim floors or by doing [Elite Dungeons](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Elite_Dungeons>).

### Token <:dungtoken:762960358930907156> farming
An efficient token <:dungtoken:762960358930907156> farming method is killing minibosses in elite dungeons, which yields 5,000 <:dungtoken:762960358930907156> per kill.
Level 70+ stats and weapons are recommended.

⬥ **Temple of Aminishi**
⬥ Tokens <:dungtoken:762960358930907156> can be farmed in the [Temple of Aminishi](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Temple_of_Aminishi>) (ed1) on the first level solo up to trio (trio recommended). Click [here](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Dungeoneering_token/Strategies>) for strategies.
    • Miniboss spawns here are random. You can stretch your minimap to check for the miniboss icons.
    • When all have been killed, restart the dungeon.
.
⬥ **Cerberus Juvenile**
⬥ Alternatively, one can kill the [Cerberus Juvenile](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Cerberus_Juvenile>) miniboss in the [Zamorakian Undercity](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Zamorakian_Undercity>) (ed4) at the beginning, solo up to 5-man. You can safespot him by standing just outside the room.
    • He always spawns.
    • By using <:bd:535532854281764884> or <:dive:1049378668197195808> you can prevent luring the mages after the Mender passes, as shown [here](<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=500nAj3PcDw>). Kill the [Chaos witch mender](<https://runescape.wiki/w/Chaos_witch_mender>) after.
    • When Cerberus has been killed, restart the dungeon.
*Note: If the Mender aggros on you as you <:dive:1049378668197195808>, the mages will be alerted!*
